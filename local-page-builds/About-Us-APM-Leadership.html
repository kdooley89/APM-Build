John Van Siclen

Chief Executive Officer

John, formerly CEO of dynaTrace (acquired by Compuware, 2011), became general manager of the Application Performance Management (APM) business unit in 2012. With over 30 years of experience in system and software companies such as NetFRAME, Informix and Interwoven, he has built a number of highly scalable businesses in some of the most competitive markets in technology. Most recently, he successfully reshaped Compuware’s APM business into the recognized leader in new generation APM through relentless focus on product innovation and customer success. CRN Magazine named Van Siclen a “Top 25 Disrupter” for his years of breaking the mold and advancing new models that drive superior customer value and accelerate business growth. The father of 4 boys, John has an AB from Princeton University and is a founding member of the New England Clean Energy executive program.

Bernd Greifeneder

Chief Technology Officer

Bernd is a serial entrepreneur and in fact invented our patented PurePath® technology. With over 15 years in software engineering, and specifically in technology performance, Bernd’s vision and insight drives Dynatrace innovation as well as new technologies such as ruxit.com, a cloud native APM and analytics solution. Besides sailing his catamaran, he also acts as an advisor to startups, speaks at entrepreneurial events and supports academic research in the application performance space. Bernd has a Master of Science from Johannes Kepler University, Linz, Austria.

Steve Tack

Vice President, Product Management

Working in technology for almost 20 years, Steve has held key roles leading the evolution and modernization of IT performance, including making Dynatrace one of the fastest growing APM solutions in the industry. He is responsible for product vision and go-to-market strategy and brings a relentless customer focus to his organization. With almost 15 years at Compuware/Dynatrace, Steve has held leadership positions in product management, support and engineering. Steve also enjoys cycling, beer and his children (not in that order). Steve holds a BA in Economics from Kalamazoo College.

Eric Fischer

Senior Vice President, Business Development

As the VP of World Wide Sales for dynaTrace (acquired by Compuware 2011), Eric scaled the organization from a $3M private company to a $300M public enterprise. With a strong background and extensive experience in both start-up and public companies, Eric brings over 20 years of IT and sales experience to Dynatrace. He’s held key executive roles at NetApp, Checkpoint, Documentum, Sybase and others. Eric also lived in Europe for 11 years and worked in international market expansion. A born competitor, he loves conquering new markets and creating high-velocity sales organizations. Graduating from the Universität Karlsruhe with a degree in industrial engineering, Eric speak fluent German.

Jonathan (Jon) Rider

Chief Information Officer

Jon brings over 25 years of technology experience to Dynatrace, including key roles as EVP of Technology and Engineering for Arcadia Solutions; CIO of Gilbane, and VP of Technology for Monster.com. A chronic over-achiever, Jon has over 20 industry certifications, including a Six Sigma Black Belt, has owned two successful consulting firms, and holds a technology patent for EHR data mining. Lisa Archer

Dan Follis

Senior VP, General Council Secretary

Dan manages the legal and contracts functions for Dynatrace. His team of 7 handles all matters involving corporate law, contracts and licensing, mergers and acquisitions, intellectual property, commercial litigation, employment law, immigration and international law. Prior to joining Dynatrace, Dan was General Counsel for Compuware and was at the forefront of a number of high profile matters including the sale to Thoma Bravo, the IPO and spin-off of Covisint, the sale and transition of 3 business units and multimillion dollar litigation against IBM for trade secret theft and antitrust violations. Prior to joining Compuware, Dan was an attorney in Chicago. He received his law degree from the University of Michigan.
